# Refund and Dispute v1

## Purpose
This document defines how refunds and disputes affect money movement on the platform.

---

## 1. Refund Principle
Refund decisions must follow:
- cancellation timing
- booking completion status
- report or dispute context
- admin review where needed

---

## 2. Refund Outcomes
Possible outcomes:
- no refund
- full refund
- partial refund

---

## 3. When Full Refund May Apply
Examples:
- helper no-show
- booking canceled by platform before fulfillment
- severe safety or trust issue validated before support delivery
- payment error that prevented valid booking

---

## 4. When Partial Refund May Apply
Examples:
- late cancellation under defined rules
- partial service delivery
- operational compromise requiring partial remediation

---

## 5. When No Refund May Apply
Examples:
- valid late cancellation by client under policy
- unsupported refund request after completed and undisputed service
- misuse of platform flow

---

## 6. Dispute Effect on Money
If a dispute is opened:
- payout may be held
- booking status may become disputed
- admin review is required
- final payout/refund decision may change

---

## 7. Operational Rule for v1
In v1, dispute review may be manual.
This is acceptable if:
- dispute status is logged
- payout can be held
- admin actions are recorded
