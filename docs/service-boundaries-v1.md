# Service Boundaries v1

## Purpose
This document defines exactly what helpers on the platform are allowed to do and what they are not allowed to do.

The platform provides non-medical support only.

These boundaries are essential for:
- trust and safety
- onboarding
- helper training
- moderation
- support decisions
- legal clarity
- client expectations

## Core Principle
Helpers provide non-medical support around healthcare-related moments.

Helpers are not doctors, nurses, paramedics, or licensed medical professionals by default through this platform.

The platform does not offer medical care.

---

## 1. Allowed Services

Helpers may provide the following types of support:

### Appointment Accompaniment
- accompany a client to an appointment
- walk with a client to a clinic, office, or practice
- help a client feel calmer and more supported on the way

### Waiting Support
- stay with the client before the appointment
- wait nearby or with the client during the process where appropriate
- provide calm, non-medical companionship

### Return-Home Accompaniment
- accompany the client after an appointment or minor procedure
- help ensure they return home safely in a non-medical sense
- help with simple navigation and presence during the return journey

### Emotional Companionship
- provide non-clinical emotional support
- help reduce stress, loneliness, or anxiety
- offer calm presence during a difficult day

### Light Paperwork Support
- help the client organize simple non-medical documents
- help with directions, appointment location details, or simple forms in a non-expert way
- help the client navigate logistics and basic information

### Light Practical Support
- carry light belongings
- help with simple errands after the appointment if agreed in advance
- help the client settle in after returning home in a non-medical way

### Communication Support
- help the client communicate in a basic, general way if there is a language barrier
- help the client understand logistics, directions, or practical next steps in a non-professional manner

---

## 2. Prohibited Services

Helpers are not allowed to perform the following tasks under any circumstance through the platform:

### Medical Advice
- giving medical advice
- recommending treatments
- explaining diagnoses as a professional
- making health decisions for the client

### Clinical Care
- nursing care
- wound care
- injections
- dressing changes
- medical monitoring
- checking clinical status as a professional

### Medication-Related Tasks
- administering medication
- deciding whether medication should be taken
- adjusting dosage
- giving injections or treatments

### Emergency Services
- acting as emergency responders
- replacing ambulance, emergency transport, or medical personnel
- handling urgent medical crises as if they are trained emergency staff

### Physical Care Beyond Safe Limits
- lifting the client in unsafe situations
- moving a client in a way that creates injury risk
- providing physical assistance that requires clinical or professional training

### Medical Transport Representation
- presenting the service as patient transport
- presenting the service as medical transport
- presenting the helper as a healthcare transport professional unless separately licensed through a future regulated structure

### Professional Claims
- claiming to be a doctor, nurse, therapist, caregiver, or paramedic unless separately verified and legally integrated into a future expanded model
- using the platform to impersonate licensed medical professionals

---

## 3. Edge-Case Rules

### If the Client Requests Medical Advice
The helper must politely refuse and state that they cannot provide medical advice.

### If the Client Appears Medically Unstable
The helper must not attempt to act as a healthcare professional. Emergency guidance and escalation rules must be followed.

### If an Emergency Occurs
The platform is not an emergency service. The helper must follow emergency instructions and contact emergency services where necessary.

### If a Client Asks for Medication Help
The helper must decline any task involving giving, adjusting, or administering medication.

### If Physical Support Becomes Unsafe
The helper must not attempt unsafe lifting, carrying, or physical handling.

### If the Request Falls Outside Platform Rules
The helper must decline or report the request.

---

## 4. Platform Safety Positioning
These rules are not optional. They are central to the platform’s identity.

The platform exists to deliver:
- practical help
- human presence
- calm accompaniment
- structured non-medical support

The platform does not exist to replace:
- hospitals
- clinics
- nurses
- doctors
- ambulances
- emergency services
