# Trust and Safety Framework v1

## Purpose
This document defines the trust and safety philosophy of the platform.

The platform connects strangers in potentially vulnerable contexts. Because of that, trust and safety is a core operating system of the business.

---

## Core Principle
The platform provides non-medical support only and must create a service environment that is:
- respectful
- transparent
- moderated
- safer than informal off-platform arrangements

---

## Trust and Safety Objectives
The trust and safety system exists to:
- reduce unsafe interactions
- prevent prohibited service behavior
- detect abusive or manipulative conduct
- support users during incidents
- remove bad actors from the platform
- reinforce trust for both clients and helpers

---

## Main Risk Categories
The platform should be designed around these risks:

### 1. Boundary Violations
Examples:
- helper gives medical advice
- helper attempts nursing-like support
- helper claims professional medical authority

### 2. Behavioral Misconduct
Examples:
- harassment
- intimidation
- discrimination
- inappropriate messages
- unsafe conduct during booking

### 3. Reliability Problems
Examples:
- no-show
- repeated late arrival
- repeated last-minute cancellations
- false availability

### 4. Financial Misconduct
Examples:
- requesting off-platform payment
- fraud
- payment manipulation
- fake booking behavior

### 5. Identity / Verification Risk
Examples:
- fake profiles
- misleading identity
- misuse of verification signals

### 6. Safety Escalation Risk
Examples:
- client appears unstable
- helper attempts prohibited physical or medical action
- emergency-like situation occurs

---

## Trust and Safety Foundation Layers
The safety system should operate in layers:

### Layer 1 — Prevention
- clear prohibited-task rules
- onboarding warnings
- helper rules acceptance
- visible service boundaries
- in-platform payments only

### Layer 2 — Visibility
- verification badges
- reviews
- response rate
- completion rate
- safety notices

### Layer 3 — Detection
- report system
- admin review
- suspicious behavior patterns
- failed reliability patterns

### Layer 4 — Enforcement
- warning
- feature restriction
- pause
- suspension
- permanent ban

### Layer 5 — Recovery
- support communication
- incident follow-up
- refund logic if needed
- review and process improvement

---

## Product Positioning Rule
The product should not imply perfect safety.
It should communicate:
- verification exists
- moderation exists
- boundaries exist
- support exists
- reporting exists
- unsafe behavior can lead to removal
