# Booking UX Rules v1

## Purpose
This document defines product and UX rules for request and booking experiences.

---

## 1. Clarity First
The user should always know:
- what service category is being booked
- when the booking happens
- where it begins
- who is involved
- how much it costs
- what helpers are allowed to do

---

## 2. Trust Must Be Visible
Every booking flow should visibly reinforce:
- non-medical support only
- verified helper information
- in-platform payment
- report/support availability

---

## 3. Short Forms, Structured Data
Forms should be:
- short enough to complete quickly
- structured enough for matching
- free of unnecessary medical detail

---

## 4. No Ambiguous Pricing
Pricing must be shown clearly before confirmation.

---

## 5. Calm UX
The booking flow should feel calm and ordered.
Avoid clutter, overly aggressive urgency, or confusing multi-column layouts.

---

## 6. Completion Confidence
After booking, users should feel:
- the booking is real
- next steps are clear
- support tools are available
